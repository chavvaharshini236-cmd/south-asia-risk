## What This Is

An analyst-grade, open-source geopolitical risk dashboard tracking political, security, and economic risk vectors across South Asia — with a focus on India's strategic neighbourhood.

Covers: India–Pakistan, India–China (LAC), Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Myanmar.

Built using an **OSINT methodology** — all data sourced from publicly verifiable, credible institutions.

---

## Key Features

- **Overall regional risk score** with trend line (2020–2026)
- **6 country risk profiles** with multi-dimensional metric bars
- **8 India strategic risk dimensions** (military, diplomatic, climate, maritime, etc.)
- **China BRI footprint** — comparative investment exposure across neighbours
- **Event timeline** — key flashpoints 2024–2026 (Operation Sindoor, Bangladesh transition, Nepal collapse)
- **Analyst assessment** — written policy commentary grounded in open-source evidence

## Methodology Note

Risk scores (0–100) are analyst-assessed composite indices. They are **not algorithmic outputs** — they reflect qualitative judgements grounded in source triangulation across multiple credible institutions. This mirrors the analytical methodology used by major risk consultancies (Control Risks, Verisk Maplecroft, Oxford Analytica).

Scores represent risk *level*, not probability of a specific event. Higher scores = greater instability / threat to India's strategic interests.
