Analyst Assessment

South Asia in 2025–26 is not experiencing a temporary spike in instability — it is undergoing a structural realignment. Three governments have fallen to youth-led uprisings since 2022. India's Neighbourhood First policy is losing ground to China's infrastructure diplomacy across every border except Bhutan. The India-Pakistan axis reached its most dangerous inflection point in decades with Operation Sindoor, and the post-ceasefire equilibrium remains brittle.

The region's underlying drivers — youth unemployment, democratic deficits, climate stress, and debt vulnerability — are structural, not cyclical. Policy responses that treat these as episodic crises will repeatedly miss the pattern. India's strategic response must address the gap between connectivity promises and connectivity delivery; between the rhetoric of Neighbourhood First and the reality of declining influence scores across its own backyard.

— Chavva Harshini, April 2026 · Policy Analyst · M.A. Political Science, Bangalore University (Top 5%)
